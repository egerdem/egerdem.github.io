Source-Weighted Scattering Delay Networks

Ege Erdem, Zoran Cvetković
King's College London, London, UK
Paper arXiv Code Interactive Demo

Abstract

Scattering Delay Networks (SDNs) are computationally efficient artificial reverberation algorithms that render room reflections in real time by connecting inter-wall delay lines via unitary, isotropic pressure scattering. They render the direct sound and first-order reflections exactly, while higher-order reflections are generated approximately through recursive scattering.

We show that the uniform source injection in SDN, inherited from digital waveguide meshes, imposes a fixed attenuation of 1/(N−1) on second-order reflections in an N-wall cuboid space. This attenuation accidentally aligns with the Image Source Method (ISM) early energy profile for boundary-adjacent sources while creating a large energy deficit for centrally located sources.

We introduce Source-Weighted SDN (SW-SDN) to address this early energy mismatch by replacing the uniform injection vector with a single-parameter weighting that controls the energy entering the recursive network from second-order reflections onward. We further propose a simple geometric predictor derived from source-to-wall distances that selects the optimal injection weight at negligible computational cost and generalises across room geometries, validated by a Monte Carlo study of 60 random source-receiver pairs across 10 rooms. The proposed rule reduces the 0–50 ms energy decay curve RMSE to within 0.5 dB of a reference ISM, while preserving the late reverberation decay rate and the low computational cost of the original algorithm.
