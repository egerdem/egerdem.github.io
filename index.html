About

I am a PhD candidate in Spatial Audio at King's College London, funded by the NMES Studentship.
I completed my M.Sc. in Multimedia Informatics at Middle East Technical University (METU), where I also worked as a Research Assistant in the Spatial Audio Research Group. Prior to that, I earned my B.Sc. in Mechanical Engineering from METU.

My research interests include sound field reconstruction, immersive audio, spatial audio signal processing, and AI applications in music and audio. I have been fortunate to work as a Visiting Researcher at Music Technology Group, UPF Barcelona and as a Research Intern at National Institute of Informatics (NII), Tokyo. During my master's studies, I worked as an Acoustical Consultant and Noise Control Engineer at Mezzo Studyo.

Research

Multimodal Learning
Self-Supervision
Audio in Metaverse

My research focuses on spatial audio and sound field reconstruction, with particular interest in deep learning approaches for immersive audio, room acoustics analysis, and AI applications in music and audio processing. I work on developing novel methods for 6DoF audio rendering, perceptual soundfield reconstruction, and microphone array calibration.
